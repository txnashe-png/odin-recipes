Chicken Ramen Recipe

Everybody Loves Ramen

This simple recipe for chicken ramen has a warm and comforting broth, and a kick from fresh ginger

Ingredients

  • 3 scallions
  • 1 tsp. honey
  • 1 Tbsp. plus ¼ cup soy sauce
  • 1 lb. skinless, boneless chicken thighs
  • 1 3" piece ginger, 2" peeled, thinly sliced, 1" peeled

Preparations

  1. Remove dark green parts from 3 scallions and slice into 2" pieces; set aside. Slice white and pale green parts into 2" pieces; set aside separately.
  2. Toss 1 lb. skinless, boneless chicken thighs, one 1" piece ginger, peeled, finely grated, 1 Tbsp. soy sauce, and 1 tsp. honey in a medium bowl to coat. Let sit 10 minutes.
  3. Heat 1 Tbsp. vegetable oil in a medium pot over medium. Cook chicken thighs, undisturbed, until golden brown underneath (it won’t be cooked through), about 5 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
  4. Cook one 2" piece ginger, peeled, thinly sliced, and reserved white and pale green scallion parts in same pot, stirring often, until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, cook 4 oz. yu choy or mini bok choy in another large pot of boiling water until bright green and tender, about 2 minutes. Using a slotted spoon, transfer to a plate. Return water to a boil and cook three 5-oz. packages fresh or dried ramen noodles according to package directions. Drain and divide among bowls.
  6. Ladle broth over noodles. Divide yu choy and 4 soft-boiled large eggs, halved, among bowls.
